Privacy Policy for WYC

Automatically-Collected Data
We, along with our service providers, utilize various technologies to collect data automatically about your interactions with our website. This automatically-collected data is used to enhance our operational capabilities, such as administering and improving the website, ensuring its functionality, and tailoring your experience with WYC.
The technologies we employ to collect this data may include, but are not limited to:
Log Files: These files gather details such as Internet Protocol (IP) addresses, browser types, geographic locations, Internet Service Providers (ISPs), referring/exit pages, operating systems, date and time stamps, and clickstream data.
Cookies: We use both persistent and session cookies to store your preferences, enhance website functionality, and personalize your browsing experience.
Flash Cookies (or Local Stored Objects): These are used to store data related to your preferences and activities, which may not be managed by the same browser settings as standard cookies.
Web Beacons: Embedded within our website or HTML-based emails, web beacons help us analyze website usage and measure the effectiveness of our electronic communications. If you prefer to receive newsletters without such technologies, you may request text-only versions.
If you make a payment for products or services on our website, the payment details you provide will be securely transmitted directly to our third-party payment processors via a secured connection.
Your browser may offer tools to reject or delete cookies. However, rejecting cookies may limit your ability to access certain features of our website. Even if you disable cookies, we may continue to collect data automatically through other technologies to improve your experience.
WYC may also work with service providers, such as Google Analytics, to collect information about your online activities and website usage patterns. To learn more about how Google Analytics collects and processes data, as well as the options available to manage your preferences, please visit Google’s Privacy Policy.

WYC Refund Policy
At WYC, we are committed to providing an exceptional experience for our participants at our events. We also recognize that circumstances may arise where participants seek clarification about refunds. This document outlines the terms and conditions regarding refunds for registrations made through our platform.
General Policy
Mandatory and Non-Refundable Registration Fee: The registration fee for scholarship categories is mandatory, non-refundable, and cannot be disputed under any circumstances.
Agreement to Terms and Conditions: Before completing the checkout process for fully and partially funded applications, all applicants are required to review and sign the declaration of terms and conditions. This step is mandatory and ensures transparency.
Refund Eligibility
Refunds will only be issued in the form of credit vouchers under specific conditions, as outlined below. These credit vouchers can be used for future WYC events.
Fully Funded and Partially Funded Applicants
The registration fee for these categories is strictly non-refundable and non-transferable.
Self-Funded Applicants
Participation in self-funded and forum admission categories is nontransferable.
Delegates who pay the full registration fee for the self-funded and forum admission category are eligible for refunds in the form of WYC Credit Voucher (CVV) under certain conditions.
In case of a refund, the registration fee for the invitation letter along with tax will be deducted from the Forum Admission and Self-Funded package. The remaining amount will be issued as a WYC Credit Voucher (CVV) in accordance with our terms and conditions.
Conditions for WYC Credit Vouchers
The full conference fee must be paid in a single transaction (not in instalments).
Requests for credit vouchers must be submitted via email at least 45 days prior to the first day of the event.
Delegates submitting requests 30 days prior will be charged a 50% cancellation fee, with the remaining amount issued as a CCV.
Delegates submitting requests 15 days prior will be charged a 75% cancellation fee, with the remaining amount issued as a CCV.
Requests made less than 15 days prior to the event will result in 100% retention of the paid amount as a cancellation fee, and no CCV will be issued.
WYC Credit Voucher Terms and Conditions
Validity: CCVs are valid for 12 months from the date of issuance.
Non-Transferable: CCVs are non-transferable, can be redeemed only once, and can only be used by the original recipient.
No Cash Value: CCVs cannot be redeemed for cash.
Partial Redemption: If the CCV value exceeds the registration fee for a future event, the remaining balance will be carried forward for future use.
Usage Limitation: CCVs can only be applied to WYC-organized events.
Special Circumstances
Force Majeure: In the event of unforeseen circumstances such as natural disasters, political instability, or other emergencies, WYC reserves the right to evaluate refund requests on a case-by-case basis.
Refund Request Process
To request a refund or credit voucher, please email support@worldyouthcentre.org  with the subject line: “Refund Request – [Event Name].” Include the following details:
Full Name
Order Number
Reason for Refund Request
Supporting Documentation (if applicable)
Travel Reimbursement Policy for Fully Funded Delegates
Fully funded delegates will be reimbursed for travel expenses after the event. Delegates must complete a reimbursement form with their bank details and other necessary information. Reimbursements will be processed within 4-8 weeks of receiving the completed form.
Invitation-Only Category
The registration fee paid to obtain an invitation letter is non-refundable and non-transferable.
Securing a visa is solely the delegate’s responsibility. WYC will only provide an invitation letter to assist with the visa process.
Contact Information
For any questions or concerns regarding this policy, please contact us at support@worldyouthcentre.org.
Policy Updates
WYC reserves the right to amend this policy at any time. Updates will be communicated through our official website.
By registering for events, you acknowledge and agree to the terms outlined in this refund policy.
Thank you for your understanding and cooperation.

WYC GDPR Compliance Policy
Introduction
The WYC is dedicated to safeguarding the privacy and personal data of all individuals, including event participants, partners, employees, and stakeholders. This GDPR Compliance Policy outlines how WYC processes, stores, and protects personal data in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) (EU) 2016/679.
Scope
This policy applies to:
Participants registering for events or conferences organized by WYC.
Employees, contractors, and volunteers involved in event management and operations.
Partners, sponsors, and vendors supporting WYC events.
Visitors and users interacting with WYC through our website or digital platforms.
This policy governs all personal data collected, processed, or stored by WYC, regardless of location or source.
What Data We Collect
CGDL collects and processes the following categories of personal data:
Personal Identification Data: Name, address, email, phone number, and passport details.
Event-Related Information: Registration details, ticket purchases, session preferences, and event participation data.
Financial Data: Payment information for event registrations and ticket sales.
Media and Marketing Data: Photographs, videos, and testimonials for promotional purposes.
Technical Data: IP addresses, cookies, and browsing activity on WYC’s website.
How We Use Personal Data
WYC processes personal data for the following purposes:
Event Registration and Management: Facilitating event participation, ticket sales, and communication with attendees.
Communication and Marketing: Sending event updates, newsletters, and promotional offers with explicit consent.
Payment Processing: Securely processing ticket purchases and managing refunds where applicable.
Compliance with Legal Obligations: Meeting legal requirements, including tax regulations and government reporting.
Media Usage: Using photographs and videos captured during events for promotional purposes across WYC’s platforms.
Legal Basis for Processing
WYC processes personal data under the following legal bases:
Consent: Explicit consent for specific purposes, such as receiving newsletters or marketing communications.
Contractual Necessity: Processing required to fulfill contractual obligations (e.g., event registration and participation).
Legal Obligation: Compliance with applicable laws and regulations.
Legitimate Interest: Processing necessary for legitimate interests, such as improving event operations and participant engagement.
How We Protect Personal Data
WYC implements robust technical and organizational measures to protect personal data from unauthorized access, loss, alteration, or misuse, including:
Encryption: Sensitive data is encrypted both in transit and at rest.
Access Control: Personal data is accessible only to authorized personnel.
Regular Audits: Conducting regular data protection audits and assessments to ensure compliance.
Data Retention Policy: Retaining personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes outlined in this policy or as required by law.
Data Subject Rights
Under the GDPR, data subjects have the following rights:
Right to Access: Request access to personal data we hold.
Right to Rectification: Request corrections to inaccurate or incomplete data.
Right to Erasure: Request deletion of personal data, subject to legal requirements.
Right to Restriction of Processing: Request restricted processing under certain circumstances.
Right to Data Portability: Receive personal data in a structured, commonly used format.
Right to Object: Object to data processing based on legitimate interests or direct marketing.
Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent to data processing at any time.
To exercise these rights, please contact us at support@worldyouthcentre.org
Third-Party Sharing and Transfers
WYC may share personal data with third parties under the following circumstances:
Event Partners and Vendors: Sharing data with event venues, sponsors, and vendors to facilitate operations.
Payment Processors: Securely sharing payment information with Stripe and other payment gateways.
Legal Obligations: Disclosing data to regulatory authorities as required by law.
International Transfers: Ensuring appropriate safeguards, such as Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), for data transfers outside the European Economic Area (EEA).
Cookies and Website Tracking
Our website uses cookies and tracking technologies to enhance user experience and analyze website traffic. You can manage your cookie preferences through your browser settings.
Data Breach Notification
In the event of a data breach, WYC will notify affected individuals and relevant authorities within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach, as required by GDPR.
Updates to this Policy
We may periodically update this GDPR Compliance Policy to reflect changes in legal requirements or business operations. Updates will be posted on our website, and significant changes will be communicated directly to participants or partners.
